Dear F&F,

We’re here in beautiful Sydney weather, ensconced in a nice condo in Loren’s complex. There are fifty steps up to our unit, only 20 more than to our front porch. To get over to Loren’s, we go down 50 steps and up 30. So we’re getting plenty of exercise, actually more than I need. By so far, my heart is still pumping. As usual, Judy complained that we were leaving too early for our flight, but I convinced her that we had passes to the United Club and could enjoy quiet and free food and a bit of luxury. But a pair of bored United hostesses informed us that the “Club” was closed because of plumbing problems. Judy was right again. No lines at check-in or CSA (no TSA at SFO). I was able to change my seat to an aisle with extra legroom and an empty middle seat, the only one I noticed. Judy was in regular economy a few rows back and enjoyed the flight and slept well.

We went immediately to Benji’s opening soccer game with a real team and he scored 3 goals and their team won, he was so proud. He’s a joy and I won’t brag about him other than to say he’s the smartest, funniest, cutest grandson since Maverick. Judy and Loren took a long walk while I napped, jet lagged. On Sunday, we bussed to the CBD (central business district) to shop for his birthday gifts. The world’s largest Lego store was amazing; Benji is a master Lego builder. Then to the newly opened humongous fish market which must have had at least 10K visitors. Benji is in school until tomorrow, so we’ve been taking long walks, the Pacific on our right, the Sydney harbor to the left with a beautiful view of the city across the water. We also went to the Museum of Contemporary Art, with impressive exhibits. All in all, a wonderful few days. Carolyn returns today from a trip to Western Australia with her friend, Mel who was on a book tour promoting her food book; she is a well known food writer in Australia. Tonight we go for the second year to her parent’s good friends for a large family seder. So all is well and this week we celebrate Loren’s 47th birthday and our 62nd anniversary.

Our best to all.

Lary and by proxy, Judy
